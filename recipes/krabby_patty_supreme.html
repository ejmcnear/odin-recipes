Krabby Patty Supreme

Description

A recreation of the Krabby Patty Supreme referred to in the popular cartoon show "Spongebob Squarepants"

Ingredients

Special Sauce

  • ⅓ cup mayo
  • ⅓ cup ketchup
  • 1 pickle, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ small onion, finely minced
  • 1-2 Tbsp yellow mustard

Krabby Patty

  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • American Cheese
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Pickle
  • Onion
  • Special sauce
  • ½ cup corn starch
  • ½ cup all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ¾ cup lager beer
  • ¼ cup vodka
  • Bacon
  • 2 quarts peanut oil

Instructions

Special Sauce

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix to combine. Season with salt and pepper if needed.

Krabby Patty

  1. Roll your ground beef into 2 ounce spheres. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add some vegetable oil to a cast iron skillet over high heat. Place two ground beef balls on the skillet. Use a cold metal spatula to press down on the meat, using a rolling pin (or something heavy) for leverage assistance.
  3. Cook until one side is nice and brown, then flip and cook for about 30 seconds before adding cheese. Stack patties on top of each other and then set aside. You will need a total of 2 double patties.
  4. Now build your burger. Bun on bottom followed by burgers and then top that with lettuce, tomato, pickles, onions, and special sauce. Repeat this again so you have a total of 4 burger patties and 3 buns. Place wooden skewers through the burger so it doesn't fall apart.
  5. In a bowl combine cornstarch along with all purpose flour, baking powder, kosher salt, lager, and vodka. Whisk to combine. Coat the burger in batter.
  6. In a large pot, heat 2 quarts of peanut oil over medium high heat to 375°F.
  7. Place your battered burger in the peanut oil and fry for about 4-6 minutes. Remove and place on a wire rack to drip excess oil and to cool off. Remove wooden skewers.
  8. Dip burger in batter again and fry for another 4-6 minutes until evenly golden brown.
  9. Remove from the oil and place on a wire rack to cool and drip any excess oil.
  10. Add a stick, serve and enjoy!
